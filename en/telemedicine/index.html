    01

    When it does help

    • Known flares that need adjustment without waiting for a full clinic visit.
    • Questions between reviews when progression can be compared through photos and history.
    • Follow-up after a recent treatment or routine change.
    • Early orientation when you need to know whether arranging clinic soon makes sense.
    See next step >

    02

    When it is not enough

    • Blurred images or symptoms that still lack enough context.
    • New lesions with rapid change that have not been examined before.
    • Intense pain, bleeding, drainage, or functional impact.
    • Likely need for dermatoscopy, procedure, or direct physical examination.
    See next step >

    03

    Move into clinic and next action

    • When the first remote read still leaves clinical doubt that should not be dragged out.
    • When the first plan does not respond as expected in the agreed review window.
    • When the case needs dermatoscopy, a procedure, or direct physical examination.
    • When the correct next step is already to open first visit instead of waiting longer online.
    See next step >

    01

    Prepare useful photos

    Natural light, steady distance, and one context photo make the first read much clearer.

    See step >

    02

    Explain what changed first

    Timing, symptoms, and previous treatment help decide whether this remote start is enough.

    See step >

    03

    Watch for switching signals

    Pain, bleeding, fast spread, or poor response are signs to move from remote care into clinic without stretching the wait.

    See step >

    04

    Open first visit

    When the decision already requires direct examination, the correct action is to open first visit instead of staying remote.
